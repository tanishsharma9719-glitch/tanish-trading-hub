Curriculum

What you will learn

Eight modules, building from how markets are structured to how you personally plan and review a trade.

01 Market Structure ＋

How price actually moves: swing highs and lows, trend versus range, and the breaks of structure that mark a real shift in control between buyers and sellers.

02 Technical Analysis ＋

Reading candles, support and resistance, and the higher-timeframe context that gives any individual setup meaning. Includes annotated walk-throughs of:

Order Blocks

A cluster of candles right before a strong, decisive move — read on the chart as the footprint of larger participants entering the market.

Liquidity Sweeps

A short, sharp move past an obvious high or low — clearing out stop orders resting there — just before price reverses in the opposite direction.

Fair Value Gaps

A three-candle imbalance left behind by a fast move — one candle's wick fails to overlap the next, leaving a visible, unfilled gap in price.

These are descriptive tools for reading a chart, not predictive signals — every pattern fails sometimes, and none of them remove the need for risk management.

03 Risk Management ＋

Position sizing, stop placement, risk-per-trade limits, and why protecting capital matters more than any single winning trade.

04 Trading Psychology ＋

Recognising revenge trading, overconfidence, and fear-driven exits — and building habits that hold up under real pressure.

05 Gold Trading Concepts ＋

What moves gold specifically: real yields, the dollar, and risk sentiment, and how its volatility differs from other markets.

06 Forex Basics ＋

Currency pairs, pip value, sessions and overlaps, and the macro calendar events that tend to move the forex market.

07 Crypto Market Fundamentals ＋

How crypto's 24/7 nature, liquidity gaps, and narrative-driven swings change the way structure and risk should be approached.

08 Trade Planning ＋

Writing a plan before entry — thesis, invalidation, target, and size — and reviewing the outcome honestly afterward.
